The idea

What we're trying to do

The approach is straightforward: acquire a home below market value, hold it for a period, identify the right buyer, and sell at a higher price — with the profit shared among everyone who comes in with us.

Risk

The main risk: timing the resale

This is a single-asset deal, so the central risk is liquidity — converting the flat back to cash at the right price, at the right time.

Selling within the planned window
A resale that runs past the target hold stretches the timeline and trims the annualised return.
Finding the right buyer at the right time
The unit needs the right buyer, not just any offer — and that can take time to line up.
Liquidity at a high ticket size
This is a large-ticket flat, so if the market isn't very liquid, moving the unit can become a little slow.
